Pareidolia is the human tendency to find meaning in ambiguity. Our mind is constantly scanning the world for patterns, rapidly matching what we see to what we already know. Even when the signal is incomplete or random, the brain prefers something over nothing.

Clouds are perhaps the most generous canvas for pareidolia. They are formless, slow-moving, and constantly becoming something else. CloudGazing is an invitation to slow down and give ourselves permission to look without purpose – to wander and imagine. In watching clouds become shapes, we glimpse how meaning itself is formed: softly, fleetingly, and always a little differently for each of us.
